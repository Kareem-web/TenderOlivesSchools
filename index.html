Creche

Through lots of exploration, guided play, group activities and interaction, children begin to build on their communication and social skills. They learn independence and are confident in their own abilities. As part of their daily activities, Children are introduced to letters sounds, numbers, shapes, colours, picture reading, pre-writing skills, and lots of story time. Expressive and creative arts, Music and lots of outdoor activities are also a part of their learning activities.

After School Care

The Reception class is the icing on the cake of Preschool. It is the final class in the Early Years Foundation Stage and understandably a very crucial class. Using the oxford reading scheme, children in this class begin to read and write simple sentences by the time they are ready to leave the reception class. In math, the 4- to 5-year-olds will be able to count 1-100 and beyond, and say what number comes before or after a given number. Children will be able to use every day language to talk about shape, size, weight, and capacity By the end of Reception, children leave this class confident, resilient, and prepared for the next phase in their learning journey.

Pre-Nursery

In Nursery class, learning is a little more structured and engaging. Children develop listening and speaking skills, independence and self-confidence. Children engage in lots of stimulating phonic activities that involve sounding of letters, blending, segmentation, as well as putting words together to make sentences. Children also explore lots of numeracy activities such as patterns, number sequence, addition and subtractions, more or less etc. Children learn to find out about, and identify some features of their environment through our various learning themes. They look closely at similarities, differences, patterns and changes through various exciting and enriching resources, tools and technology..
